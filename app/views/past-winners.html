CARLOS MURILLO

Carlos Murillo is a playwright, director and educator. His plays include: "Your Name Will Follow You Home," "Augusta and Noble," "Mayday Mayday Tuesday," "A Thick Description of Harry Smith," "Diagram of A Paper Airplane," "Dark Play or Stories for Boys," "Mimesophobia," "A Human Interest Story," "Offspring of the Cold War," "Schadenfreude" and others. They have been produced throughout the US and Eastern Europe. In New York, his work has been seen at P73, NYC Summer Play Festival, En Garde Arts, New Dramatists, NY Theatre Workshop and the Public Theater’s New Work Now!

MARCO ANTONIO RODRÍGUEZ

Barceló con hielo

MARCO ANTONIO RODRÍGUEZ

Born and raised in New York City, with roots from the Dominican Republic, Marco is a graduate of La Guardia High School for the Performing Arts in NYC and holds a Master of Fine Arts degree from Southern Methodist University. His play, "La luz de un cigarrillo," received an extended by popular demand run at New York's Off-Broadway LATEA theater, Lehman Stages and the Dominican Republic's Teatro Las Máscaras (where it currently has an open ended run). It is the recipient of 5 HOLA, 4 ACE and 3 prestigious Soberano awards, including Outstanding Achievement In Playwriting and directing.

VINCENT TORO

21

VINCENT TORO

Vincent Toro is a Puerto Rican poet, playwright, performer, director, and educator with a MFA in Creative Writing from Rutgers University. From 2006-2011, Mr. Toro was Director of the Theater Program at The Guadalupe Cultural Arts Center in San Antonio, Texas. He has taught creative writing for Gemini Ink in San Antonio, The Teachers and Writers Collaborative in NY, and the Dreamyard Drama Project in the Bronx. His play "Oil on Canvas" won 2nd prize in the 2009 "Metlife Nuestras Voces" Latino Playwriting Competition and has been staged at the Guadalupe Cultural Arts Center, Repertorio Español, and Teatro La Tea. His play "The Teacher’s Lounge" was also staged by Teatro La Tea and by The Hunt's Point CDC in the Bronx.
