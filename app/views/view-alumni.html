DIEGO M. CHIRI

Fin de Semana

DIEGO M. CHIRI

Diego M. Chiri is a Peruvian actor, writer, and director living in New York. Chiri graduated from Community College of Philadelphia (2009) and Bucknell University (2012) with Cum Laude honors and degrees in Film and Theatre, with a concentration in Stage Direction. Chiri has directed “Ashes” (2010), “The Zoo Story” (2011) and the documentary “Devising the American Family” (2012), the latter of which was filmed at the Edinburgh Fringe Festival. Chiri has also contributed to the devised productions of “The Bacchae” (2011) and “Really Trying Here” (2011). In New York, Chiri has directed “Fin de semana” (2014) at Repertorio Español and the stage reading of “Gazoline” (2015) at IATI Theater. Chiri is a member of Phi Theta Kappa Honor Society and he has received the Jack Kent Cooke Foundation Scholarship, the CCP Foundation Scholarship, and recently the Van Lier Fellowship. Chiri works at TimeLine Video, a media production agency in New York.

VICTORIA COLLADO

EL CRUCE SOBRE EL NIÁGARA

VICTORIA COLLADO

Born in Miami, FL, Victoria Collado is a Cuban-American actress and director influenced by the works of Bertolt Brecht, Pina Bausch, and Agusto Boal. She has an interest in merging the movement world with naturalist plays in order to create exciting theatrical experiences. Victoria received her BFA in Theatre Performance from Florida International University. She has directed for MicroTeatro Miami, What If Works, and other theaters in the Miami Area. The Van Lier production “El cruce sobre el Niágara” was her New York directing premiere. Her work on “Los últimos días de una casa” garnered an ACE award nomination and an ATI award win for Best Director of a Monologue. Victoria is honored to be a Van Lier Fellow and hopes that the legacy of the fellowship continues for years to come.

ESTEFANÍA FADUL

ASÍ VAN LOS FANTASMAS DE MÉXICO, PRIMERA PARTE

ESTEFANÍA FADUL

Estefanía Fadul is a Colombia-born, New Hampshire-raised director and producer. Directing credits include: “Así van los fantasmas de México” by Matthew Paul Olmos (Repertorio Español Van Lier Fellowship), “Well” by Lisa Kron (Pleiades Productions), “Underneath” by Christine Jean Chambers (Around the Block), and “A Streetcar Named Desire” (Vassar College). She has assistant directed at the Public Theater, Oregon Shakespeare Festival, MCC, New York Stage and Film, and The Flea. Estefanía recently assistant produced Soho Rep.’s first all-ages show, “Washeteria.” She is the founding Artistic Director of Pleiades Productions, a 2015 Drama League Directors Project Fall Fellow, and a member of the Lincoln Center Theatre Directors Lab. B.A. from Vassar College.
